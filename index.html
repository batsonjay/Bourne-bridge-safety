Why the atâ€‘grade plan is risky

  • Everyday use will surge: Once a safe, practical crossing exists, path traffic will increase sharply as residents and visitors adopt it for regular rides and walks.
  • Event-scale safety risks: More than a dozen organized rides each year â€” including the Pan-Mass Challenge and Best Buddies â€” would generate continuous crossings, heightening the chance of vehicleâ€“cyclist conflicts.
  • Downhill approach hazards: Riders descending from the bridge will face group slowdowns or stops at the crossing, creating avoidable crash potential.
  • Unclear connection to destinations: Most users must immediately cross Sandwich Rd to reach the south-side Canal Bikeway, compounding exposure to moving traffic.

Benefits of an overpass

Rather than forcing the path to descend steeply to meet Sandwich Rd at grade, a multimodal overpass could take advantage of the existing elevation on the south side, providing a safe elevated crossing that connects more directly to the Cape Cod Canal bikeway.

  • Eliminates vehicleâ€“human conflict.
  • Accommodates event surges (Pan-Mass, Best Buddies) without closing Sandwich Rd.
  • Safer for children and families accessing the ice rink and Canal path.
  • Supports over a dozen large (e.g. PMC) organized group rides per year crossing the Cape Cod canal.
  • Encourages increased year-round use by locals and visitors.
